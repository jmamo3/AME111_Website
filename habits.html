03 — SAFE BROWSING HABITS

Stay safe wherever you browse

Good security isn’t just about passwords — it’s about the habits you build every day. These simple practices dramatically reduce your risk online.

Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)

2FA adds a second layer of protection, making it far harder for attackers to access your accounts even if they have your password.

Look for HTTPS

Before entering sensitive information, check for https:// and a lock icon in the address bar. This ensures your connection is encrypted.

Be careful on public Wi‑Fi

  • Avoid logging into important accounts
  • Use a VPN if possible
  • Never access banking or email on open networks

⚠️ Attackers can intercept traffic on public Wi‑Fi. Treat it as untrusted.

Keep your software updated

Updates patch vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit. Turn on automatic updates whenever possible.

What to do if your account is compromised

  • Change your password immediately
  • Enable 2FA
  • Check recent login activity
  • Revoke access to suspicious devices or apps

💡 Small habits add up. The more consistent you are, the safer you stay.
