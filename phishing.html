02 — PHISHING & SOCIAL ENGINEERING

Don't take the bait

Phishing is the #1 way attackers gain access to accounts and systems. It doesn't require hacking — just tricking you into handing over your credentials yourself.

What is phishing?

Phishing is when an attacker impersonates a trusted entity — your bank, your university, a coworker — to trick you into clicking a malicious link, entering your password, or downloading malware. According to Proofpoint's State of the Phish report, over 3.4 billion phishing emails are sent every single day.

⚠️ Phishing doesn't just happen over email — it also happens via text message (smishing), phone calls (vishing), and even fake websites that look identical to real ones.

Red flags to look for

Urgency"Your account will be closed in 24 hours" — pressure to act fast without thinking.
Generic greeting"Dear user" or "Dear customer" instead of your actual name.
Suspicious senderThe email looks official but the address is something like support@amaz0n-help.com.
Sketchy linksHover over links before clicking — the real URL often doesn't match what's displayed.
Unexpected attachmentsYou weren't expecting a file. Don't open it.
Too good to be trueYou won something. You're getting a refund. Someone left you money.

What to do if you clicked something

  • Don't enter any information on the page that opened
  • Change your password for that account immediately
  • Enable 2FA if you haven't already
  • Run a malware scan (Malwarebytes is free)
  • Report it — forward phishing emails to reportphishing@apwg.org

When in doubt, don't click. Go directly to the website by typing the URL yourself instead of following any link in an email.
