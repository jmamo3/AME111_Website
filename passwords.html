01 — PASSWORD HYGIENE

Stronger passwords, safer accounts

Weak or reused passwords are the easiest way for attackers to break into your accounts. The good news: with a password manager, strong security becomes effortless.

Why strong passwords matter

Attackers don’t “guess” passwords — they use massive databases of leaked credentials and automated tools that can try millions of combinations per second. If you reuse a password anywhere, a single breach can compromise every account that shares it.

⚠️ Over 24 billion passwords have been leaked online in the past decade. If you reuse passwords, odds are one of yours is already out there.

What makes a strong password?

  • At least 14–16 characters long
  • A mix of letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Not based on personal info (birthdays, pets, sports teams)
  • Unique for every single account

💡 You don’t need to remember strong passwords — let a password manager generate and store them for you.

Use a password manager

Password managers create long, random passwords and autofill them for you. They also alert you if a site is breached or if your password is weak or reused.

  • Bitwarden (free & open source)
  • 1Password
  • LastPass

What to do if a password was exposed

  • Change the password immediately
  • Enable 2FA on the account
  • Check if your email appears in breaches using Have I Been Pwned
  • Update any other accounts that reused the same password
